About

Getting Involved

If you're a New York City resident and would like to see a change in your local street tree landscape, take a look at the NYC Parks Tree Service Request system! And if you'd like to get more involved, there are opportunities to become a tree steward or join volunteer events.

Reading the Charts

The radar charts presented on this website represent the different street tree plantings around the city, and when in the year they are at their peak bloom. Our radar chart has six spokes, going from early spring to late summer. The number of trees in peak bloom at a given time is plotted on the axis formed by that spoke, with the 0 point of each axis at the center of the chart. The different graphs on this website have different scales based on the overall number of trees in the neighborhood, borough, or entire city that is being plotted. The trees are grouped by the color of their flowers and each colors if plotted separately, forming several different "petals" on each graph.
